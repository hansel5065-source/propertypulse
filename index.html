🔍 Skip Tracing Tips

  • Use owner mailing address from tax records (often different from property address)
  • Check social media (Facebook, LinkedIn) for owner contact info
  • Send yellow letters to distressed owners — handwritten style gets 3-5% response rate
  • Best pitch: "I can close in 2 weeks, cash, as-is — no repairs, no agents, no hassle"
  • Follow up 3 times minimum — most deals close on the 2nd or 3rd contact

⚖ NC vs SC Foreclosure Rules

North Carolina

  • 10-day upset bid period after foreclosure sale
  • Highest bidder wins — must raise bid by 5% or $750 (whichever greater)
  • No redemption period after upset bid window closes
  • Tax foreclosures go through courts — check Meck, Gaston, Union clerks

South Carolina

  • Master-in-Equity sales, typically 1st Monday monthly
  • 30-day redemption period — debtor can reclaim property
  • Surplus funds go to debtor (can be motivation to negotiate pre-sale)
  • York County — check Master-in-Equity office for schedules

Both states: Always verify title before bidding. Check for IRS liens, HOA liens, and municipal code violations.

💰 Exit Strategies

Fix & Flip

Best for SFR under $200K ARV. Timeline: 3-6 months. Target 20%+ ROI after all costs.

Buy & Hold

Best for rentable properties in good school districts. Target 1% rule (monthly rent = 1% of purchase).

Wholesale

Assign contract to another investor for $5-15K fee. Lowest risk, no rehab needed, fast turnaround.

Subject-To

Take over existing mortgage payments. Best when owner is behind but has equity. Creative financing play.

Quick Flip (Buy & Resell)

Buy the property at auction or direct from owner, then resell immediately to another investor at a markup. No rehab — just acquire below market and flip the deal. Best for tax sales and foreclosures with clear title.
