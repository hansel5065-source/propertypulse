👨‍💼 Pre-Foreclosure Strategy — The Outreach Game

Pre-foreclosures are 4-5x the volume of actual foreclosures (~1,000 vs ~200) and usually carry better margins. They have no fixed sale date because the bank hasn't auctioned yet — the goal is to buy DIRECT from the owner before that happens.

When to Engage Pre-Foreclosures

  • Equity ≥ 50% — Owner has room to negotiate (sell to you, pay off bank, walk with cash)
  • Tag includes "High Equity" or "Free and Clear"
  • Tag includes "Absentee Owners" or "Out-of-State Owners" — less attached, easier sale
  • Skip Low Equity (under 30%) — no margin to negotiate

Workflow

1Filter Pre-FC tab → Equity Yes → Sort by Equity %
2Skip-trace owner (TruePeopleSearch / BeenVerified — 5 min/owner)
3Outreach: yellow letter, postcard, or cold call. 3-5% response rate
4Negotiate: cash, close in 14 days, owner walks with $X
5Follow up 3+ times — deals close on the 2nd or 3rd contact

Pitch Script (yellow letter / call)

"I noticed you may be behind on payments at [address]. I'm a local investor and I buy houses cash, as-is. I can close in 14 days, pay off your mortgage, and put real money in your pocket — far better than letting the bank take it through foreclosure. No agents, no fees, no repairs needed. Can we talk?"

Pre-FC vs Foreclosure — Which to Chase?

Pre-Foreclosure

  • ~1,000+ leads in app
  • No fixed deadline — months of runway
  • Direct negotiation
  • Interior access usually granted
  • Cleaner title (no junior-lien surprise)
  • Higher margin (30-50%+ possible)
  • Requires outreach + patience

Foreclosure (Auction)

  • ~200 leads in app
  • Fixed sale date — show up or miss it
  • Public auction at courthouse
  • No interior access
  • Junior liens may surface at sale
  • Tighter margin (10-15% typical)
  • Cash-only, fast

Run both: Pre-FC outreach for the pipeline, foreclosures for occasional courthouse wins. Most successful Charlotte-metro investors do 80% pre-foreclosure / 20% auctions.

🔍 Skip Tracing Tips

  • Use owner mailing address from tax records (often different from property address)
  • Check social media (Facebook, LinkedIn) for owner contact info
  • Send yellow letters to distressed owners — handwritten style gets 3-5% response rate
  • Best pitch: "I can close in 2 weeks, cash, as-is — no repairs, no agents, no hassle"
  • Follow up 3 times minimum — most deals close on the 2nd or 3rd contact

⚖ NC vs SC Foreclosure Rules

North Carolina

  • 10-day upset bid period after foreclosure sale
  • Highest bidder wins — must raise bid by 5% or $750 (whichever greater)
  • No redemption period after upset bid window closes
  • Tax foreclosures go through courts — check Meck, Gaston, Union clerks

South Carolina

  • Master-in-Equity sales, typically 1st Monday monthly
  • 30-day redemption period — debtor can reclaim property
  • Surplus funds go to debtor (can be motivation to negotiate pre-sale)
  • York County — check Master-in-Equity office for schedules

Both states: Always verify title before bidding. Check for IRS liens, HOA liens, and municipal code violations.

💰 Exit Strategies

Fix & Flip

Best for SFR under $200K ARV. Timeline: 3-6 months. Target 20%+ ROI after all costs.

Buy & Hold

Best for rentable properties in good school districts. Target 1% rule (monthly rent = 1% of purchase).

Wholesale

Assign contract to another investor for $5-15K fee. Lowest risk, no rehab needed, fast turnaround.

Subject-To

Take over existing mortgage payments. Best when owner is behind but has equity. Creative financing play.

Quick Flip (Buy & Resell)

Buy the property at auction or direct from owner, then resell immediately to another investor at a markup. No rehab — just acquire below market and flip the deal. Best for tax sales and foreclosures with clear title.
